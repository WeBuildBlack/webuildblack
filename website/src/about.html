About We Build Black

How It All Started

How It All Started

In 2016, Devin Jackson founded Black Software Engineers of New York City (BSENYC) — a Meetup group that brought together Black software engineers for networking, learning, and community building. What started as a small meetup quickly grew into something bigger.

The community's energy and hunger for more resources, more programs, and more representation made it clear: this needed to be more than a meetup. In November 2017, We Build Black was officially incorporated as a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization in Brooklyn, NY.

Since then, WBB has expanded into workforce training programs, youth coding initiatives, an annual conference, and a thriving Slack community of Black technologists across the country.

Where We're Headed

We're building the infrastructure for Black excellence in tech. Through education, mentorship, and industry partnerships, we're creating a pipeline that takes people from their first line of code to their first six-figure salary — and beyond.
