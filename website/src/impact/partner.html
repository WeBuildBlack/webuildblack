Build a More Equitable Tech Industry

What We Look For

Diversity isn't a nice-to-have, it's imperative. These benchmarks are signals that help us distinguish where companies are at on the path toward equity. Use them to identify opportunities to affect systemic change within your business.

1

Employee Resource Group

Having ERGs gives employees of targeted identities a safe space to be heard and take action on the change they would like to see in and out of the workplace and are key to creating a culture of belonging.

2

Dedicated D&I Leadership

No person should ever be paid for one job and have to do another on top of it. Having a dedicated D&I leader or team ensures that employees can focus on mastering their crafts. Inclusion isn't a side-of-desk task.

3

Black Owned

Building Black is at the core of our mission. We support Black businesses by hiring and highlighting them. The vendors and services you use every day are also opportunities for change.

4

Transparent Demographics

Publishing diversity statistics is a signal that you recognize there is a problem. It lets the public know you're working toward a solution and have a reference for your progress.

5

Transparent Salary Bands

Wage inequality is a long-standing issue across industries. Unless there is salary transparency, efforts to build a more representative workforce will only widen the gap.

6

External Activism

Your commitment to inclusion shouldn't only be internal. Investment in community organizations is key to building a diverse pipeline. Support programs that broaden exposure to technical career paths.

7

Black Executives

From executives to individual contributors, Black people need to have a seat at the table. A company's leadership is indicative of its broader diversity perspective.

8

Internship/Apprenticeship Program

A large number of the Black community coming into tech are coming in at the ground level. Open internships bridge the experience gap for less traditional candidates.

9

Mentorship Program

A big aspect of D&I work is not only hiring but retaining that diverse talent. Structured mentorship programs give employees a guiding hand in navigating their careers.
