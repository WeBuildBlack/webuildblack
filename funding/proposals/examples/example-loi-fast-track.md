# Letter of Inquiry — Fast Track Workforce Development Program

**Date:** 2026-03-04
**To:** Robin Hood Foundation
**From:** We Build Black, Inc.
**Re:** Request for $150,000 to support the Fast Track workforce development program

---

## About We Build Black

We Build Black (WBB) is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit founded in 2017 in Brooklyn, NY, dedicated to empowering the Black community to achieve socio-economic change through technical education and professional development. Originally started as the Black Software Engineers of NYC Meetup in 2016, WBB has grown into a multi-program organization serving hundreds of community members annually through workforce training, youth education, conference programming, and career support.

## The Need

Black professionals remain underrepresented in the tech industry, comprising only 5% of the technical workforce despite making up 13% of the U.S. population. In New York City, where the tech sector generates over $100 billion annually, this gap represents both a social justice issue and a missed economic opportunity. Traditional training programs often fail to address the financial barriers that prevent career changers from completing technical training — participants must choose between learning and earning, making completion rates unsustainably low.

## Program Description

Fast Track is a 6-month workforce development program that provides structured technical training pathways in Android Development, UX Design, and Data Analytics. What distinguishes Fast Track is its milestone-based stipend model: participants progress through 6 milestones, earning $1,000 at each stage, totaling $6,000 per participant upon completion. This design directly addresses the financial barriers that cause most workforce programs to see high dropout rates.

Participants are matched with industry mentors, receive career coaching, and build portfolio projects that demonstrate job readiness. Past cohorts have partnered with companies like Shopify to create direct pathways from training to employment.

## Request

We are requesting $150,000 to fund one full cohort of 15 Fast Track participants over 6 months, covering:
- Participant stipends ($90,000)
- Program management and mentorship ($35,000)
- Technology, curriculum, and operations ($25,000)

## Expected Outcomes

- 15 participants complete technical training across 3 tracks
- 80% completion rate (vs. industry average of ~50%)
- 70% job placement within 6 months of program completion
- Average salary increase of $25,000+ for placed participants
