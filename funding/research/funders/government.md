# Government Grant Programs

> Federal, New York State, and NYC government funding relevant to WBB.
> Government grants typically require more administrative capacity but offer larger, multi-year funding.
> Last updated: 2026-03-04

## Federal — National Science Foundation (NSF)

### Broadening Participation in Computing (BPC)
- **Program**: NSF CISE directorate funds projects that increase diversity in computing
- **Relevant solicitations**:
  - **INCLUDES (Inclusion across the Nation of Communities of Learners of Underrepresented Discoverers in Engineering and Science)**: Large-scale collaborative grants to broaden participation
  - **CSforAll: Research and RPPs**: Funds researcher-practitioner partnerships for CS education. WBB could partner with a university.
  - **ITEST (Innovative Technology Experiences for Students and Teachers)**: Youth STEM engagement — Crowns of Code fit
- **Typical Range**: $100,000 - $500,000 over 2-3 years
- **Fit**: Strong for Crowns of Code and Fast Track, but NSF grants typically require a research component. WBB would need a university partner (e.g., NYU, Brooklyn College, Medgar Evers College).
- **Application**: Grants.gov; requires SAM.gov registration and UEI
- **Timeline**: Solicitations posted annually; 6-12 month review cycle
- **Key Requirement**: Research design, evaluation plan, and often a university PI (principal investigator)

### Action Items
- [ ] Register WBB on SAM.gov and obtain UEI (required for all federal grants)
- [ ] Identify a university partner for research collaboration (Medgar Evers College CUNY is in Brooklyn and has CS programs)
- [ ] Monitor NSF solicitations at nsf.gov/funding

## Federal — Department of Labor (DOL)

### Employment and Training Administration (ETA) Grants
- **Program**: DOL funds workforce training programs for underserved populations
- **Relevant programs**:
  - **YouthBuild**: Grants for pre-apprenticeship programs serving opportunity youth (16-24). Could fund Crowns of Code expansion or a new youth workforce track.
  - **H-1B Skills Training Grants**: Training U.S. workers in industries that use H-1B visas (tech). Directly funds tech workforce programs.
  - **Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) formula grants**: Flow through state/local workforce boards. WBB could become an eligible training provider under WIOA.
  - **Registered Apprenticeship grants**: DOL funds organizations creating registered apprenticeship programs in tech. Fast Track could evolve into a registered apprenticeship.
  - **Reentry Employment Opportunities (REO)**: For justice-involved individuals — relevant if WBB serves this population.
- **Typical Range**: $100,000 - $1,000,000+ over 2-4 years
- **Fit**: Very strong for Fast Track. DOL explicitly funds tech workforce training for underrepresented communities. YouthBuild could fund youth programming.
- **Application**: Grants.gov; DOL posts Funding Opportunity Announcements (FOAs)
- **Key Requirement**: Outcome tracking (job placement, wage data, retention), partnership with employers, serving eligible populations

### Action Items
- [ ] Explore becoming a WIOA-eligible training provider through NYC's workforce system
- [ ] Monitor DOL ETA grant announcements at dol.gov/agencies/eta/grants
- [ ] Investigate registered apprenticeship designation for Fast Track

## Federal — Department of Education (DOE)

### Relevant Programs
- **Title IV-A (Student Support and Academic Enrichment)**: Flows through school districts; WBB could partner with NYC DOE for Crowns of Code
- **Education Innovation and Research (EIR)**: Grants for innovative education models with evidence of effectiveness. Competitive.
- **Fund for the Improvement of Postsecondary Education (FIPSE)**: Supports innovative postsecondary education models
- **21st Century Community Learning Centers**: After-school and summer programs — Crowns of Code fit, but funds flow through NY State Education Dept
- **TRIO Programs**: If WBB participants are first-generation college students or low-income
- **Typical Range**: $50,000 - $500,000
- **Fit**: Moderate. DOE grants typically flow through schools or colleges. WBB would need to partner with NYC DOE or a CUNY school.

### Action Items
- [ ] Explore partnership with NYC DOE for Crowns of Code as an after-school provider
- [ ] Investigate 21st Century Community Learning Centers through NY State

## New York State

### Department of Labor — Workforce Development Program (WDP)
- **Program**: NY DOL funds training programs through community colleges and nonprofits
- **What it covers**: Occupational training, support services, job placement
- **Typical Range**: $50,000 - $500,000
- **Fit**: Strong for Fast Track. WDP grants fund exactly this kind of training.
- **Application**: Through NY DOL; sometimes administered through SUNY/CUNY
- **Note**: Often requires partnership with a CUNY or SUNY institution

### Empire State Development (ESD) — Workforce Programs
- **Program**: ESD occasionally funds workforce training initiatives tied to economic development
- **Fit**: Moderate; more oriented toward employer-driven training

### NY State Education Department
- **21st Century Community Learning Centers**: Federal pass-through for after-school STEM programs
- **Adult Education grants**: For basic skills and workforce preparation
- **Fit**: Moderate for youth programs (Crowns of Code) and adult workforce (Fast Track)

### NY State Office of Children and Family Services (OCFS)
- **Program**: Youth development program funding
- **Fit**: Could fund Crowns of Code if structured as youth development

### Action Items
- [ ] Contact NY DOL about WDP eligibility for Fast Track
- [ ] Explore 21st Century CLC funding through NY State Ed Dept
- [ ] Identify CUNY partner for state workforce grants (City Tech, Medgar Evers, or BMCC all in Brooklyn/Manhattan)

## New York City

### Department of Youth and Community Development (DYCD)
- **Program**: NYC's primary funder of youth programs
- **Relevant programs**:
  - **Work, Learn, Grow (WLG)**: Summer and year-round youth employment. Crowns of Code could become a WLG provider.
  - **Summer Youth Employment Program (SYEP)**: NYC's marquee summer jobs program. WBB could host SYEP participants.
  - **Comprehensive After-School System of NYC (COMPASS)**: After-school programming for middle and high school students.
  - **Capacity-building grants**: Organizational development funding for small/mid-size nonprofits
  - **Community Solutions RFPs**: Regular RFPs for community-based services
- **Typical Range**: $50,000 - $300,000 per year
- **Fit**: Very strong for youth programming. DYCD is the most accessible city funding source for Crowns of Code. SYEP hosting requires minimal infrastructure.
- **Application**: Respond to DYCD RFPs at nyc.gov/dycd; PASSPort (NYC procurement system) registration required

### NYC Small Business Services (SBS)
- **Program**: Workforce development contracts
- **Relevant programs**:
  - **Workforce1 Career Centers**: WBB could become a training vendor for Workforce1
  - **NYC Tech Talent Pipeline**: City initiative to grow the local tech workforce — WBB could become a partner
  - **Training provider contracts**: SBS contracts with organizations to deliver job training
- **Typical Range**: $25,000 - $200,000
- **Fit**: Strong for Fast Track. The NYC Tech Talent Pipeline is specifically designed for organizations like WBB.
- **Application**: Through NYC SBS; PASSPort procurement system

### NYC Council — Discretionary Funding
- **Program**: Each NYC Council member has discretionary funds (~$500K-$1M+ per year) to allocate to nonprofits in their district
- **Brooklyn District**: WBB at 147 Front Street is in City Council District [confirm district number — likely District 33 or 35]
- **Typical Range**: $5,000 - $50,000
- **Fit**: Accessible entry point for city funding. Low barriers, but requires relationship with council member.
- **Application**: Contact the local council member's office directly. Submit a funding request during budget season (typically January-March).

### Brooklyn Borough President's Office
- **Program**: Discretionary grants and capital funding for community organizations
- **Typical Range**: $5,000 - $25,000
- **Application**: Submit request during annual budget process

### NYC Economic Development Corporation (NYCEDC)
- **Program**: LifeSci NYC, Cyber NYC, and other industry-specific workforce initiatives
- **Fit**: Moderate; if WBB aligns with a specific NYCEDC industry initiative

### NYC Department of Education (DOE) Partnerships
- **Program**: Partnership programs where nonprofits deliver enrichment in public schools
- **Fit**: Strong for Crowns of Code — deliver coding in Brooklyn public schools
- **Application**: Through iZone, CS4All initiative, or direct school partnerships

## Federal Grant Readiness Checklist

Before applying for any federal grant, WBB needs:

- [ ] **SAM.gov registration**: Required for all federal grants. Free but takes 2-4 weeks.
- [ ] **Unique Entity Identifier (UEI)**: Replaces DUNS number. Obtained through SAM.gov.
- [ ] **Grants.gov account**: Online portal for federal grant applications.
- [ ] **Indirect cost rate**: Negotiate with a federal agency or use the 10% de minimis rate.
- [ ] **Financial management systems**: Ability to track federal funds separately, maintain records for audits.
- [ ] **Board resolution**: Authorizing the ED to apply for and accept federal funds.
- [ ] **Audit readiness**: Organizations spending $750K+ in federal funds require a Single Audit (A-133).

## City/State Grant Readiness Checklist

- [ ] **PASSPort registration**: NYC's procurement system. Required for all city contracts and grants. nyc.gov/passport
- [ ] **Prequalification**: Many NYC agencies require nonprofits to be prequalified in PASSPort.
- [ ] **Charities registration**: Registered with NY Attorney General's Charities Bureau.
- [ ] **Workers' compensation and disability insurance**: Required for most government contracts.
- [ ] **NYC Vendex/questionnaire**: Background check for organizations receiving city funds.

## Priority Ranking

1. **NYC DYCD** — Most accessible; youth programs are a natural fit; fastest path to city funding
2. **NYC SBS / Tech Talent Pipeline** — Direct workforce development alignment
3. **NYC Council discretionary** — Low barrier, relationship-based; start here for first city dollars
4. **DOL H-1B/workforce grants** — Large funding, strong fit, but competitive and admin-heavy
5. **NY State WDP** — Workforce training focus; needs CUNY partner
6. **NSF BPC / ITEST** — Large funding for STEM education; needs university partner
7. **NYC DOE CS4All** — Partnership for Crowns of Code in schools
