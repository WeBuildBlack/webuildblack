## Tier 1 — High Alignment

### Salesforce / Salesforce.org

- **Giving Focus**: Workforce development, STEM education, equality, community engagement
- **Relevant Programs**:
  - Salesforce.org grants for workforce development nonprofits
  - Trailhead workforce development partnerships (free training platform — could complement Fast Track curriculum)
  - 1-1-1 model: 1% equity, 1% product, 1% employee time donated
  - Salesforce Futureforce (internship program — potential hiring partner)
  - Community grants through local Salesforce offices (NYC presence)
- **Typical Range**: $25,000 - $250,000
- **Fit with WBB**: Strong. Salesforce explicitly prioritizes workforce development for underrepresented communities. Trailhead partnership could provide curriculum for a Salesforce admin/developer Fast Track track. NYC office presence makes local engagement possible.
- **Approach**: Apply through Salesforce.org grants portal. Also pitch Trailhead curriculum integration with Fast Track. Propose Salesforce employees as Fast Track mentors through 1-1-1 volunteer hours.
- **Website**: salesforce.org

## Corporate Engagement Beyond Grants

Not all corporate relationships need to start with a grant ask. Other engagement models:

1. **Event sponsorship**: Mavens I/O Conference is a natural entry point. Sponsors get brand visibility, recruiting access, and speaking opportunities.
2. **Hiring partnerships**: Companies commit to interviewing/hiring WBB program graduates. Lower cost than grants; high value for both sides.
3. **Employee volunteering**: Corporate employees serve as mentors, mock interviewers, or workshop facilitators. Free for WBB, counts as CSR for the company.
4. **In-kind support**: Free tools, platform licenses, cloud credits, office space for events.
5. **Curriculum partnerships**: Companies provide training content or certifications that WBB integrates into programs (e.g., AWS certifications through Fast Track).
6. **Speaking/recruiting at Mavens I/O**: Companies pay to sponsor and recruit at the conference.

### Recommended Sequencing
For new corporate relationships, consider this progression:
1. Start with in-kind support or employee volunteering (low commitment, builds relationship)
2. Move to event sponsorship (Mavens I/O, The Bridge kickoff)
3. Then propose program funding or hiring partnership
4. Eventually pitch multi-year program partnership with shared outcomes
