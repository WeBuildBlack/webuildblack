## Robin Hood Foundation

### Overview
New York City's largest poverty-fighting organization. Funds, partners with, and provides capacity-building support to ~250 NYC nonprofits. Known for rigorous metrics and evidence-based grantmaking.

### Focus Areas
- Workforce development and job training
- Education (K-12 and postsecondary)
- Economic security and mobility
- Survival (housing, food, health)

### Typical Grant Size
$50,000 - $500,000 annually; multi-year grants common for proven programs

### Fit with WBB
- **Very strong**: WBB is a Brooklyn-based nonprofit serving low-income Black New Yorkers with workforce training and education — directly in Robin Hood's strike zone
- Fast Track (paid workforce training with stipends) and Crowns of Code (youth STEM) are strong candidates
- Robin Hood values measurable outcomes (job placement rates, wage increases) — WBB should build robust tracking

### Application Info
- Does not accept unsolicited proposals; uses a proactive sourcing model
- Best approach: Get introduced through a Robin Hood grantee or board connection
- Attend Robin Hood events, build visibility in NYC nonprofit ecosystem
- If invited, expect deep due diligence on financials, outcomes data, and organizational capacity
- Website: robinhood.org

### Action Items
- [ ] Identify connections to Robin Hood grantees or board members
- [ ] Prepare outcomes data package (job placements, wage changes, completion rates)
- [ ] Attend Robin Hood public events for visibility

## Ford Foundation

### Overview
One of the world's largest private foundations (~$16B endowment). Focuses on reducing inequality and strengthening democratic values globally. Known for large, multi-year grants.

### Focus Areas
- Future of Work(ers) — how technology changes work, who benefits
- Racial equity and justice
- Technology and society
- Civic engagement and governance

### Typical Grant Size
$100,000 - $1,000,000+; multi-year grants (2-3 years)

### Fit with WBB
- **Strong**: Ford's Future of Work(ers) and racial equity priorities align well with WBB's workforce programs
- Ford specifically funds organizations led by and serving communities of color
- The Foundation values systemic change — WBB's pipeline model (youth to workforce to career) tells a systemic story
- Competitive: Ford receives thousands of proposals; WBB would need polished outcomes data

### Application Info
- Accepts letters of inquiry through online portal
- Often funds through intermediary organizations or collaborative initiatives
- Building relationships with Ford program officers is important
- Website: fordfoundation.org
- Look for: Technology & Society program, Future of Work(ers) initiative

### Action Items
- [ ] Review current Ford grantees in workforce development and racial equity
- [ ] Draft letter of inquiry emphasizing pipeline approach and systemic impact
- [ ] Identify Ford program officers working on Future of Work(ers)

## Kapor Center

### Overview
Founded by Mitch and Freada Kapor. Dedicated to making the tech ecosystem more diverse and inclusive. Both a foundation (Kapor Foundation) and a research/advocacy org (Kapor Center). Based in Oakland, CA but funds nationally.

### Focus Areas
- Diversity and inclusion in tech (specifically racial equity)
- STEM education for underrepresented groups
- Gap-closing education models
- Tech workforce diversity pipeline

### Typical Grant Size
$25,000 - $250,000; Kapor Capital also makes impact investments

### Fit with WBB
- **Very strong — one of the best mission matches in this list**
- Kapor's entire thesis is closing gaps in tech representation for Black and Latinx communities
- WBB's full program suite (youth STEM, workforce training, interview prep, community building) is exactly the pipeline Kapor funds
- Kapor values community-led organizations serving their own communities

### Application Info
- Check Kapor Foundation website for open RFPs
- Kapor Center hosts Levers for Change conference — good networking opportunity
- Small team, so warm introductions matter
- Website: kaporcenter.org, kaporfoundation.org

### Action Items
- [ ] Review Kapor Foundation's recent grantees
- [ ] Apply when RFP opens; emphasize community-led model and pipeline approach
- [ ] Attend Kapor Center events for relationship building

## W.K. Kellogg Foundation

### Overview
One of the largest U.S. foundations (~$8B endowment). Headquartered in Battle Creek, MI. Strong focus on racial equity as an organizing principle across all grantmaking.

### Focus Areas
- Racial equity (explicit priority across all programs)
- Education and learning
- Community engagement
- Thriving children and families

### Typical Grant Size
$100,000 - $1,000,000; multi-year grants

### Fit with WBB
- **Strong**: Kellogg's centering of racial equity aligns with WBB's identity as a Black-led, Black-serving organization
- Crowns of Code (youth) and Fast Track (economic mobility) fit their education and thriving children priorities
- Kellogg funds organizations that address root causes of inequity, not just symptoms

### Application Info
- Proactive grantmaking model — they find grantees more than they accept applications
- Some open RFPs posted periodically
- Getting on their radar requires visibility in racial equity and education spaces
- Website: wkkf.org

### Action Items
- [ ] Monitor wkkf.org for open RFPs
- [ ] Build visibility in racial equity nonprofit networks
- [ ] If invited to apply, emphasize community-driven, root-cause approach

- Racial equity and social justice

- Emerson Collective team members are active at education and social impact conferences

- Less focus on racial equity specifically, more on economic opportunity broadly

### Application Info
- Check schultzfamilyfoundation.org for current programs and RFPs
- Onward initiative partners with intermediary organizations
- Website: schultzfamilyfoundation.org

### Action Items

- [ ] Assess whether Fast Track participants fit the "opportunity youth" definition

## Lumina Foundation

### Overview
Indianapolis-based foundation focused on increasing the proportion of Americans with post-secondary credentials. ~$1.5B endowment.

### Focus Areas
- Post-secondary credential attainment (degrees, certificates, credentials)
- Workforce-aligned learning
- Equity in education (racial, socioeconomic)
- Alternative credential models

### Typical Grant Size
$50,000 - $300,000

### Fit with WBB
- **Moderate to strong**: Fast Track's milestone-based workforce training model aligns with Lumina's interest in alternative credentialing
- WBB could frame Fast Track completions as workforce credentials
- Lumina is interested in closing racial gaps in credential attainment

### Application Info
- Invitation-based grantmaking; also publishes open RFPs
- Focus on research and systems change as much as direct service
- Website: luminafoundation.org

### Action Items
- [ ] Frame Fast Track in credentialing language (milestones as micro-credentials)
- [ ] Monitor Lumina RFPs for workforce innovation or equity-focused calls
- [ ] Consider whether WBB can offer recognized credentials (stackable certificates)

## Thurgood Marshall College Fund

### Overview
National organization supporting students at HBCUs and PBIs (Predominantly Black Institutions). Based in DC.

### Focus Areas
- HBCU student support and scholarships
- Black professional development
- Corporate partnerships for Black talent pipeline

### Typical Grant Size
Primarily scholarships; program partnerships $25,000 - $100,000

### Fit with WBB
- **Moderate**: More of a partnership opportunity than a traditional grant funder
- WBB could partner to extend programming to HBCU students (virtual Bridge cohorts, Fast Track tracks)
- TMCF's corporate partners (who fund scholarships) could also fund WBB programs

### Application Info
- Not a traditional grantmaker — approach as a partnership
- Website: tmcf.org

### Action Items
- [ ] Explore HBCU student recruitment for The Bridge (virtual program)
- [ ] Identify TMCF corporate partners who might also fund WBB
