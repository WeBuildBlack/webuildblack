# Community Foundations & Local Funders

> Brooklyn/NYC community foundations, local funders, and small grant programs.
> These are often the most accessible first grants for growing nonprofits.
> Last updated: 2026-03-04

---

## Brooklyn Community Foundation

### Overview
Brooklyn's dedicated community foundation. Grants exclusively to Brooklyn-based organizations. Strong focus on racial equity since 2020 strategic shift.

### Focus Areas
- Racial justice (explicit priority)
- Youth development
- Education
- Economic opportunity
- Community organizing

### Typical Grant Size
$10,000 - $75,000; one-year grants with renewal possible

### Fit with WBB
- **Very strong**: WBB is Brooklyn-based, Black-led, and focused on education and economic opportunity — hits every priority
- Brooklyn Community Foundation specifically funds small-to-mid-size Brooklyn nonprofits led by people of color
- Crowns of Code (Brooklyn youth) and Fast Track (Brooklyn workforce) are natural fits

### Application Info
- Open RFP process; grants cycle typically opens in spring
- Website: brooklyncommunityfoundation.org
- Relatively straightforward application compared to large foundations
- They also offer capacity-building support for grantees

### Action Items
- [ ] Monitor grant cycle opening (typically March-May)
- [ ] Attend Brooklyn Community Foundation events for relationship building
- [ ] Apply for general operating support or program-specific grant

## New York Community Trust (NYCT)

### Overview
One of the oldest and largest community foundations in the U.S. Grants to NYC-area nonprofits. Manages over $3B in assets.

### Focus Areas
- Education and youth development
- Workforce and economic security
- Health and human services
- Arts and culture
- Community development

### Typical Grant Size
$25,000 - $150,000; competitive grants and donor-advised fund distributions

### Fit with WBB
- **Strong**: NYCT funds workforce development and education nonprofits in NYC. Competitive but accessible.
- NYCT has specific funds for Black-led organizations and racial equity work

### Application Info
- Competitive grants posted on website; rolling applications for some programs
- Website: nycommunitytrust.org
- Also manages donor-advised funds — some DAF holders may independently fund WBB

### Action Items
- [ ] Review current NYCT grant opportunities
- [ ] Apply for workforce development or education grants
- [ ] Explore whether any DAF holders have been flagged for racial equity giving

## Fund for the City of New York (FCNY)

### Overview
Improves the performance of government and nonprofits in NYC. Provides fiscal sponsorship, grants, and capacity building.

### Focus Areas
- Social innovation
- Government and nonprofit effectiveness
- Youth development
- Workforce

### Typical Grant Size
$10,000 - $100,000

### Fit with WBB
- **Moderate**: More of a capacity-building partner than a traditional funder. Could help WBB strengthen operations, evaluation, or technology.
- If WBB needs fiscal sponsorship for a specific project, FCNY is an option.

### Application Info
- Website: fcny.org
- Approach for capacity-building partnerships or project-specific funding

### Focus Areas

- Education (cradle to career)

### Typical Grant Size

## Con Edison

### Overview
NYC's largest energy utility. Runs community giving program focused on STEM education and workforce development in its service area.

### Focus Areas
- STEM education (K-12)
- Workforce development (energy sector and general)
- Environmental justice
- NYC community development

### Typical Grant Size
$10,000 - $75,000

### Fit with WBB
- **Moderate**: STEM education focus aligns with Crowns of Code. Workforce development aligns with Fast Track. Con Edison specifically funds NYC nonprofits.
- Con Edison is a major NYC employer and values local workforce pipeline programs.

### Application Info
- Community grants through online portal
- Website: coned.com/en/about-us/community-affairs
- Application typically opens annually

### Action Items
- [ ] Apply for STEM education grant for Crowns of Code
- [ ] Explore workforce partnership (Con Edison hires tech workers)

## Brooklyn Navy Yard Development Corporation (BNYDC)

### Overview
Manages the Brooklyn Navy Yard, a 300-acre industrial park near WBB's location. Runs workforce and community programs.

### Focus Areas
- Local hiring and workforce development
- STEM education for Brooklyn youth
- Community engagement in DUMBO/Navy Yard area

### Typical Grant Size
$5,000 - $25,000 (community grants); larger workforce contracts

### Fit with WBB
- **Moderate**: WBB at 147 Front Street is very close to the Navy Yard. Geographic proximity strengthens the case. Navy Yard tenants include tech companies that could hire WBB graduates.

### Application Info
- Website: brooklynnavyyard.org
- Contact community programs team directly
- May offer event space, workforce partnerships, or direct grants

## Donor-Advised Funds (DAFs)

DAFs are a growing source of charitable giving. Individuals hold DAFs at institutions like Fidelity Charitable, Schwab Charitable, and NYCT. WBB can attract DAF grants by:

1. Being visible on charity databases (GuideStar/Candid profile, Charity Navigator)
2. Being listed on DAF platforms (Fidelity Charitable, Schwab Charitable allow donors to search for nonprofits)
3. Having a strong online presence that DAF holders find when searching for racial equity, STEM education, or Brooklyn nonprofits

### Action Items
- [ ] Create/update GuideStar (Candid) profile with financials, programs, and impact data
- [ ] Verify WBB appears on Fidelity Charitable, Schwab Charitable, and other DAF search tools
- [ ] Ensure website donate page mentions DAF giving as an option
