## Short Mission (for character-limited forms)

## Origin Story

We Build Black began in 2016 as the Black Software Engineers of NYC Meetup, a grassroots gathering for Black technologists in New York City. Recognizing the need for sustained, structured support beyond networking events, founder Devin Jackson incorporated We Build Black as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit in November 2017. Since then, WBB has grown from a meetup group into a multi-program organization delivering workforce training, youth coding education, interview preparation, and an annual conference — all centered on closing the racial gap in the technology industry.

## Vision

A tech industry where Black professionals are equitably represented, compensated, and empowered at every level.

### One-paragraph description

With support from partners including Google, JP Morgan Chase, Shopify, and Microsoft, WBB has served [X,XXX]+ community members and trained [XXX]+ participants in technical skills that lead to employment in the technology industry.

### Problem statement
Black professionals remain significantly underrepresented in the U.S. technology sector, holding approximately 8% of tech jobs despite comprising 13% of the population. In New York City, one of the world's largest tech hubs, Black workers face persistent barriers to entry including lack of access to training, professional networks, and culturally competent career support. The cost of coding bootcamps ($10,000-$20,000+) puts many pathways out of reach, and once employed, Black technologists often lack the peer support systems needed to navigate predominantly white workplaces and advance into senior roles.

### Solution statement
We Build Black addresses these barriers through a pipeline approach: introducing Black youth to coding (Crowns of Code), training adults for tech careers with stipend support (Fast Track), preparing job seekers for the interview process (The Bridge), convening the community annually (Mavens I/O), and providing ongoing peer support through community meetups and a dedicated Slack workspace. All programs are free to participants and designed by and for the Black community.
