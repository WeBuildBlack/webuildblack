## Landscape Overview

New York City has one of the deepest nonprofit funding ecosystems in the country. City government alone distributes billions annually to nonprofits through contracts and grants. Combined with NYC-focused foundations, corporate giving programs, and community philanthropy, WBB has access to a rich local funding landscape that many organizations outside NYC do not.

### Why NYC Location Matters for Funding

1. **NYC government is a major funder**: City agencies (DYCD, SBS, DOE, HRA) contract with hundreds of nonprofits. Becoming part of this system provides recurring revenue.
2. **NYC foundations prioritize local**: Robin Hood, NYCT, Brooklyn Community Foundation, Tiger Foundation, and others fund exclusively or primarily in NYC.
3. **Corporate headquarters density**: Google, JPMorgan, Microsoft, Amazon, Meta, Salesforce, and hundreds of other tech companies have NYC offices with local community giving.
4. **Brooklyn brand**: Brooklyn has cultural cachet. "Brooklyn-based nonprofit" carries meaning with funders, media, and corporate partners.
5. **DUMBO specifically**: WBB's DUMBO location places it in one of Brooklyn's tech hubs. DUMBO is home to Etsy (formerly), Two Sigma, and many startups. This geography is relevant for employer partnerships and local corporate giving.

## NYC Government Funding Sources

### NYC Department of Youth and Community Development (DYCD)
- **What it funds**: Youth programs, community development, workforce programs
- **Key programs for WBB**:
  - SYEP (Summer Youth Employment Program): Host youth in tech roles
  - COMPASS/SONYC: After-school programming
  - Work, Learn, Grow: Year-round youth employment
  - Capacity-building grants for small nonprofits
- **Typical range**: $50,000-$300,000/year
- **How to access**: RFPs posted on PASSPort (NYC procurement system)
- **Priority**: HIGH — most accessible city agency for WBB

### NYC Small Business Services (SBS)
- **What it funds**: Workforce development, job training, career services
- **Key programs for WBB**:
  - NYC Tech Talent Pipeline: City initiative to grow local tech workforce
  - Workforce1 training vendor contracts
  - Industry partnership programs
- **Typical range**: $25,000-$200,000/year
- **How to access**: Through SBS directly; some programs are RFP-based
- **Priority**: HIGH — Tech Talent Pipeline is a near-perfect match

### NYC Department of Education (DOE)
- **What it funds**: Partnerships with nonprofits to deliver education in schools
- **Key programs for WBB**:
  - CS4All: Bring CS education to all NYC schools. WBB could deliver Crowns of Code in Brooklyn schools.
  - Community Schools: Wraparound services at selected schools
  - CTE (Career and Technical Education) partnerships
- **How to access**: Through DOE vendor partnerships or school-level partnerships
- **Priority**: MEDIUM — valuable for Crowns of Code but requires school relationships

### NYC Council Discretionary Funding
- **What it is**: Each City Council member allocates discretionary funds to nonprofits in their district
- **WBB's district**: [Confirm district — 147 Front Street DUMBO is likely Council District 33 (Lincoln Restler) or District 35]
- **Typical range**: $5,000-$50,000
- **How to access**: Direct relationship with council member; submit funding request during budget season (Jan-March)
- **Priority**: HIGH — lowest barrier to entry; start here for first city dollars

### Brooklyn Borough President's Office
- **What it funds**: Discretionary grants for Brooklyn community organizations
- **Typical range**: $5,000-$25,000
- **How to access**: Annual budget request process
- **Priority**: MEDIUM — small but builds local political relationships

### NYC Economic Development Corporation (NYCEDC)
- **What it funds**: Industry-specific workforce initiatives, innovation programs
- **Key programs**: Cyber NYC, LifeSci NYC, NYCEDC venture and innovation programs
- **How to access**: Through NYCEDC partnerships; some RFPs
- **Priority**: LOW-MEDIUM — relevant only if WBB aligns with a specific NYCEDC initiative

## NYC Participatory Budgeting

NYC Council members allocate a portion of their capital budget through participatory budgeting (PB), where community members vote on how to spend funds. While PB typically funds physical infrastructure (parks, school equipment), there is occasionally opportunity to fund technology equipment for community organizations or public spaces.

- **How it works**: Community members propose and vote on projects in their district
- **Typical allocation**: $1M per participating district
- **WBB opportunity**: Propose technology equipment (laptops, projectors, networking equipment) for Crowns of Code or community programming space
- **Cycle**: Annual; proposals typically due in fall, voting in spring

## Recommended NYC Funding Sequence

For a Brooklyn-based nonprofit at WBB's stage, this is the recommended progression through NYC funding:

### Phase 1: Foundation (Now)
1. **NYC Council discretionary**: Meet local council member, request $10K-$25K for Crowns of Code or community programming
2. **Brooklyn Borough President**: Submit budget request for $5K-$15K
3. **Brooklyn Community Foundation**: Apply for next grant cycle ($10K-$50K)
4. **Google for Nonprofits + Microsoft for Nonprofits**: Enroll for free tools (not cash, but reduces operating costs)

### Phase 2: Growth
5. **NYC DYCD SYEP**: Host summer youth employment participants
6. **NYC SBS / Tech Talent Pipeline**: Become recognized workforce partner
7. **Robin Hood Foundation**: Build relationship; seek introduction through existing grantee
8. **New York Community Trust**: Apply for competitive grants

### Phase 3: Scale
9. **NYC DYCD COMPASS**: Contract for after-school Crowns of Code programming
10. **NYC DOE CS4All**: Partner to deliver CS in Brooklyn schools
11. **NYC DYCD capacity-building**: If available, apply for organizational development support
12. **Pinkerton Foundation / Tiger Foundation**: Multi-year program support

### Phase 4: Institutional
13. **WIOA eligible training provider**: Become certified for public workforce funding
14. **NYC government contracts**: Compete for workforce training contracts through SBS
15. **State workforce grants**: Through CUNY/SUNY partnerships

## Key Registrations and Compliance

To access NYC/NY State funding, WBB needs:

- [ ] **PASSPort registration**: NYC procurement system — required for all city grants and contracts
- [ ] **PASSPort prequalification**: Required for most DYCD and other agency funding
- [ ] **NY Charities Bureau registration**: Required for all nonprofits soliciting donations in NY State
- [ ] **NYC Vendex**: Background disclosure for organizations receiving city funds
- [ ] **Workers' comp and disability insurance**: Required for government contracts
- [ ] **SAM.gov**: For federal pass-through funding (some city grants use federal funds)
- [ ] **NYS Grants Gateway**: For state-level funding (ny.gov grants)
