## II. Organization Background

### History & Mission

We Build Black was born in 2016 as the Black Software Engineers of NYC Meetup, a grassroots gathering for Black technologists in New York City. In November 2017, WBB incorporated as a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization, formalizing its mission: **to empower the Black community to achieve socio-economic change through technical education and professional development.**

Headquartered at 147 Front Street, Brooklyn, NY 11201, WBB has grown from a meetup group into a multi-program organization serving the Black tech community across New York City and beyond.

### Programs

- **Fast Track** — A workforce development program with six progressive milestones, each tied to a $1,000 stipend. Participants pursue tracks in Android Development, UX Design, or Data Analytics, supported by industry mentors and hiring partners.
- **Crowns of Code** — A youth coding program introducing young people in the Black community to programming, computational thinking, and technology careers.
- **The Bridge** — An 8-week virtual interview accountability program where participants are organized into pods of 4-5 by target role, logging weekly progress in DSA practice, mock interviews, and job applications.
- **Mavens I/O** — WBB's flagship technology conference bringing together Black technologists, industry leaders, and aspiring professionals for talks, workshops, and networking.
- **She Builds Black** — A dedicated chapter for women, offering workshops, mentorship, and community events tailored to the experiences of Black women in tech.
- **Community Meetups** — Regular gatherings for networking, skill-sharing, and community building.

### Past Support & Partnerships

WBB has been supported by Google, JP Morgan Chase, Birchbox, The New York Times, Shopify, and Microsoft, among others. These partnerships have included direct funding, employee mentorship, event sponsorship, and hiring pipeline development.
